Kenya PAYE tax bands for 2026

Kenya taxes employment income (PAYE) progressively: each band's rate applies only to the income that falls within it, not to your whole salary. After the tax is worked out, every resident employee subtracts a flat personal relief of KES 2,400 a month (KES 28,800 a year).

Questions

Is this my full take-home pay?

No — this is your PAYE income tax only. Your actual net salary is also reduced by statutory deductions: NSSF, the Social Health Insurance Fund (SHIF, 2.75% of gross), and the Affordable Housing Levy (1.5% of gross). Add those to get take-home pay. This tool isolates the income-tax portion so you can see the bands clearly.

What is personal relief?

A flat KES 2,400 per month (KES 28,800 per year) that every resident employee subtracts from their calculated PAYE. It is a credit against tax, not a deduction from income, which is why low earners pay no PAYE at all.

How does insurance relief work?

If you pay for qualifying life or health insurance, you get relief worth 15% of the premium, capped at KES 5,000/month (KES 60,000/year). Enter your premium under "Add pension & insurance relief" to include it.

Do the bands really jump at 500,000 and 800,000?

Yes. The Finance Act 2023 added a 32.5% band on monthly income between KES 500,001 and 800,000, and a 35% band above KES 800,000. Some older calculators still show only four bands (35% above 500,000) — that is out of date and overstates tax for high earners.
