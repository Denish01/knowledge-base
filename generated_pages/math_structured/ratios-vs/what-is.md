# What is Ratios Vs?

Ratios vs proportions is a mathematical concept that refers to the comparison of two or more quantities, often used to describe the relationship between different parts of a whole.

A ratio is a way of expressing the relationship between two quantities, often denoted by the use of a colon. For example, a ratio of 2:3 indicates that for every 2 units of one quantity, there are 3 units of another quantity. This concept is used to compare the sizes of different groups or quantities. Ratios can be used to describe a wide range of real-world phenomena, from the mixing of ingredients in a recipe to the distribution of resources in a community.

Proportions, on the other hand, are a type of equation that states that two ratios are equal. A proportion can be written as a statement, such as "a is to b as c is to d", or as an equation, such as a/b = c/d. Proportions are often used to solve problems that involve equivalent ratios, such as scaling a recipe up or down, or determining the amount of a certain ingredient needed to achieve a specific ratio. Understanding the difference between ratios and proportions is essential for working with mathematical relationships and making accurate calculations.
