# What is Types Of Percentages?

## INTRODUCTION
Classification of percentages is a fundamental concept in mathematics, covering various types that are used to describe proportions, fractions, and decimals as parts of a whole. Understanding the different types of percentages is crucial in real-life applications, such as finance, science, and social sciences, as it enables individuals to accurately calculate, compare, and interpret data. The classification of percentages matters because it provides a framework for organizing and analyzing information, allowing for more effective decision-making and problem-solving. By recognizing the distinct types of percentages, individuals can better comprehend complex concepts and apply mathematical principles to solve problems.

## MAIN CATEGORIES
The following are the primary types of percentages, each with its unique characteristics and applications:
1. **Percentage Increase**
   - Definition: A percentage increase represents the growth or rise in a quantity from its original value to a new value, expressed as a percentage of the original value. It is used to measure the magnitude of change in a quantity over time.
   - Key Characteristics: Involves a comparison between two values, with the new value being greater than the original value.
   - Example: If a product's price rises from $100 to $120, the percentage increase is 20%, indicating a 20% growth in price.
2. **Percentage Decrease**
   - Definition: A percentage decrease represents the reduction or decline in a quantity from its original value to a new value, expressed as a percentage of the original value. It is used to measure the magnitude of reduction in a quantity over time.
   - Key Characteristics: Involves a comparison between two values, with the new value being less than the original value.
   - Example: If a company's sales fall from $1000 to $800, the percentage decrease is 20%, indicating a 20% decline in sales.
3. **Percentage Change**
   - Definition: A percentage change represents the difference between two values, expressed as a percentage of the original value. It can be either an increase or a decrease, depending on whether the new value is greater than or less than the original value.
   - Key Characteristics: Encompasses both percentage increases and decreases, providing a comprehensive measure of change.
   - Example: If a stock's price changes from $50 to $60, the percentage change is 20%, indicating a 20% increase in value.
4. **Composite Percentage**
   - Definition: A composite percentage represents the combination of two or more percentage changes, resulting in a net percentage change. It is used to calculate the overall effect of multiple successive changes.
   - Key Characteristics: Involves the multiplication of successive percentage changes to obtain the net effect.
   - Example: If a product's price increases by 10% and then decreases by 5%, the composite percentage change can be calculated to determine the net effect on the price.
5. **Percentage Points**
   - Definition: Percentage points represent the absolute difference between two percentages, used to measure the change in a percentage value. It is not a percentage of the original value but rather a difference between two percentage values.
   - Key Characteristics: Involves subtracting one percentage value from another to obtain the difference in percentage points.
   - Example: If a survey shows that 25% of respondents prefer product A and 30% prefer product B, the difference is 5 percentage points, indicating a 5% greater preference for product B.

## HOW THEY RELATE
The different types of percentages are interconnected and can be used in various contexts to analyze and interpret data. Percentage increases and decreases are specific cases of percentage change, which provides a broader measure of change. Composite percentages are used to calculate the net effect of successive changes, while percentage points provide a measure of the difference between two percentage values. Understanding how these types of percentages relate to one another is essential for accurate calculations and informed decision-making.

## SUMMARY
The classification system for types of percentages includes percentage increase, percentage decrease, percentage change, composite percentage, and percentage points, each with its unique characteristics and applications, providing a comprehensive framework for analyzing and interpreting proportional data.
