The Pythagorean theorem is a mathematical concept that describes the relationship between the lengths of the sides of a right-angled triangle. It states that the square of the length of the hypotenuse, which is the side opposite the right angle, is equal to the sum of the squares of the lengths of the other two sides. This concept has numerous applications in various fields, including architecture, engineering, and physics, where it is used to calculate distances, heights, and lengths of objects.

In geometry, the Pythagorean theorem is often compared to other concepts, such as similarity and congruence of triangles, to understand the properties and relationships between different shapes. The theorem is also used in conjunction with other mathematical principles, like trigonometry and algebra, to solve problems involving right-angled triangles.
