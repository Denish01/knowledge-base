# What is Price To Earnings Ratio For Beginners?

1. WHAT YOU'LL LEARN: This guide will teach you the basics of the price to earnings ratio, a simple and powerful tool used to evaluate stocks. By the end of this guide, you will have a solid understanding of what the price to earnings ratio is and how to use it to make informed investment decisions.

2. THE BASICS: The price to earnings ratio, also known as the P/E ratio, is a way to measure how much investors are paying for each dollar of profit a company makes. It is calculated by dividing the current stock price by the company's earnings per share. Think of it like a report card for stocks, helping you understand if a stock is fairly priced or not.

3. KEY TERMS: Here are a few important terms to know when learning about the price to earnings ratio:
- Earnings: The profit a company makes after paying all its expenses.
- Stock price: The current price of a single share of a company's stock.
- Share: A small part of a company that you can buy or sell.
- Investor: A person who puts money into stocks, hoping to make a profit.
- Profit: The money a company makes after paying all its expenses.

4. FIRST STEPS: To get started with the price to earnings ratio, you need to understand the basics of stocks and how they work. You should also learn how to find a company's earnings per share and current stock price, which can usually be found online. Start by looking up the P/E ratio of a few well-known companies to get a feel for how it works.

5. COMMON BEGINNER MISTAKES: Here are a few common mistakes to avoid when learning about the price to earnings ratio:
- Not understanding what the P/E ratio means, and thinking it is the only factor to consider when evaluating a stock.
- Comparing the P/E ratio of companies in different industries, which can be misleading.
- Not considering other important factors, such as the company's growth prospects and financial health.
- Thinking that a high P/E ratio always means a stock is overpriced, or that a low P/E ratio always means a stock is underpriced.

6. NEXT STEPS: Once you have a solid understanding of the price to earnings ratio, you can start to learn about other tools and techniques for evaluating stocks. You may want to learn about other financial ratios, such as the dividend yield or the return on equity. You can also start to explore different investment strategies, such as value investing or growth investing. Remember, the key to successful investing is to do your research, be patient, and never stop learning.
