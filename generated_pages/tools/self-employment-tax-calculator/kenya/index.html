How Kenya Self Employment Tax Works

Kenya's self-employment tax system is designed for individuals who earn income from their own businesses or freelance work. The tax rates and brackets are as follows:

  • 10% on the first KES 235,001 - 332,000
  • 15% on KES 332,001 - 388,000
  • 20% on KES 388,001 - 564,000
  • 25% on KES 564,001 and above.

These rates apply to the tax year 2023.

How to Use This Calculator

To use the self-employment tax calculator, you need to input your net earnings from self-employment. This includes income from your business or freelance work, minus any business expenses.

For example, let's say you're a freelance writer with a net income of KES 800,000. You would enter this amount into the calculator to determine your self-employment tax liability.

The calculator will then apply the tax rates and brackets to your net earnings, taking into account any deductions or exemptions you may be eligible for.

Key Kenya Self Employment Tax Rules

Some key rules to keep in mind when it comes to self-employment tax in Kenya include:

  • The tax year in Kenya runs from January 1 to December 31.
  • You are required to file your tax return by June 30 of the following year.
  • You may be eligible for deductions on business expenses, such as equipment, rent, and travel costs.
  • If your net earnings from self-employment are below KES 235,000, you are exempt from paying self-employment tax.

Filing Deadlines and Exemptions

It's essential to be aware of the filing deadlines and exemptions that apply to your specific situation. For instance, if you're a resident of Kenya, you're required to file a tax return annually, regardless of your income level. However, if you're a non-resident, you may only be required to file a tax return if you have income sourced in Kenya.

Common Questions

What is the tax year in Kenya?

The tax year in Kenya runs from January 1 to December 31.

How do I calculate my net earnings from self-employment?

To calculate your net earnings, subtract your business expenses from your total income.

What deductions can I claim as a self-employed individual in Kenya?

You can claim deductions on business expenses, such as equipment, rent, and travel costs.

Do I need to file a tax return if I'm a non-resident of Kenya?

You may only be required to file a tax return if you have income sourced in Kenya.

Can I file my tax return electronically?

Yes, you can file your tax return electronically through the Kenya Revenue Authority (KRA) website.
