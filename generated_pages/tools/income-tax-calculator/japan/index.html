How Japan Income Tax Works

The Japanese income tax system is a progressive tax system, meaning that higher income earners are taxed at a higher rate. The tax year in Japan runs from January 1 to December 31. As of the 2022 tax year, the tax rates and brackets are as follows:

  • 5% on income up to ¥1.95 million
  • 10% on income between ¥1.95 million and ¥3.95 million
  • 20% on income between ¥3.95 million and ¥9 million
  • 23% on income between ¥9 million and ¥18 million
  • 33% on income between ¥18 million and ¥40 million
  • 40% on income above ¥40 million
  • 45% on income above ¥55 million (special high-income tax rate)

Yes, you can claim a tax deduction for your mortgage interest in Japan, up to a certain limit (¥650,000 per year).
