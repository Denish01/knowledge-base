How Kenya Income Tax Works

The Kenya income tax system is a progressive tax system, where the tax rate increases as the taxable income increases. The Kenya Revenue Authority (KRA) is responsible for collecting income tax from individuals and companies. The current income tax rates in Kenya range from 10% to 30%. The tax year in Kenya runs from January 1 to December 31.

The tax brackets for the 2022 tax year are as follows:

  • 10% on taxable income up to KES 147,580
  • 15% on taxable income between KES 147,581 and KES 286,311
  • 20% on taxable income between KES 286,312 and KES 406,764
  • 25% on taxable income between KES 406,765 and KES 570,849
  • 30% on taxable income above KES 570,849

Key Kenya Income Tax Rules

Here are some key rules to keep in mind when it comes to income tax in Kenya:

  • The tax year in Kenya runs from January 1 to December 31.
  • The deadline for filing individual tax returns is June 30 of the following year.
  • Employers are required to deduct income tax from their employees' salaries and remit it to the KRA on a monthly basis.
  • Self-employed individuals are required to file their tax returns on a quarterly basis.
  • Taxpayers can claim exemptions and deductions, such as pension contributions and mortgage interest, to reduce their taxable income.

Common Questions

What is the deadline for filing individual tax returns in Kenya?

The deadline for filing individual tax returns in Kenya is June 30 of the following year.

Do I need to file a tax return if I am employed?

Yes, you will still need to file a tax return, even if you are employed, if you have other sources of income or if you want to claim exemptions and deductions.

Can I claim exemptions and deductions on my tax return?

Yes, you can claim exemptions and deductions, such as pension contributions and mortgage interest, to reduce your taxable income.

How do I pay my income tax in Kenya?

You can pay your income tax through the KRA's online portal, or through a bank or other authorized payment agent.

What happens if I fail to file my tax return or pay my income tax on time?

If you fail to file your tax return or pay your income tax on time, you may be subject to penalties and interest on the amount owed.
